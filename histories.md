# World History: Year 1 to 2026
A chronological record of the most important events in human history, organized by century.

## Structure
Each century covers the most significant incidents with source references.

---

## 1-100 AD

1. **Birth of Christianity** — Jesus of Nazareth is crucified in Jerusalem (~30-33 AD), leading to the foundation of Christianity, which becomes the world's largest religion. Source: Tacitus, *Annals* (15.44); Flavius Josephus, *Antiquities of the Jews* (18.3.3)

2. **Eruption of Mount Vesuvius (79 AD)** — Destroys the Roman cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum, killing thousands. Preserved under ash, providing unparalleled archaeological evidence of Roman life. Source: Pliny the Younger, *Letters* (6.16, 6.20)

3. **Roman Empire reaches greatest extent under Trajan (98 AD)** — Marcus Ulpius Trajanus becomes Emperor, initiating a period of maximum territorial expansion. Source: Cassius Dio, *Roman History* (68)

4. **Spread of Buddhism to China** — Buddhist missionaries arrive in China via the Silk Road during the Han Dynasty. Source: *Hou Hanshu* (Book of the Later Han)

5. **Invention of paper in China (~105 AD)** — Cai Lun credited with refining papermaking technique using mulberry bark, hemp, and rags. Revolutionizes information storage. Source: *Hou Hanshu* (Book of the Later Han)

## 101-200 AD

1. **Roman Empire under the Five Good Emperors (96-180 AD)** — Nerva, Trajan, Hadrian, Antoninus Pius, and Marcus Aurelius rule in succession, considered Rome's golden age of stability and prosperity. Source: Edward Gibbon, *The Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire*; Cassius Dio

2. **Bar Kokhba Revolt against Rome (132-136 AD)** — Jewish rebellion in Judea crushed by Emperor Hadrian. Results in mass displacement of Jewish population and renaming of Judea to Syria Palaestina. Source: Cassius Dio, *Roman History* (69.12-14)

3. **Hadrian's Wall constructed (~122 AD)** — Defensive wall across northern Britain, marking the northwest frontier of the Roman Empire. 73 miles long. Source: *Historia Augusta*, Life of Hadrian

4. **Spread of Christianity throughout Roman Empire** — By 200 AD, Christian communities exist in every major city. Despite periodic persecution, the religion grows exponentially. Source: Tertullian, *Apology*; Eusebius, *Ecclesiastical History*

5. **Han Dynasty begins to collapse (184 AD)** — Yellow Turban Rebellion weakens the Han Dynasty, leading to the Three Kingdoms period. Source: *Records of the Three Kingdoms* (Sanguozhi)

## 201-300 AD

1. **Crisis of the Third Century (235-284 AD)** — Roman Empire nearly collapses from civil war, plague, economic crisis, and barbarian invasions. Over 20 emperors in 50 years. Source: Cassius Dio; *Historia Augusta*

2. **Three Kingdoms Period in China (220-280 AD)** — China split into Wei, Shu, and Wu after the fall of the Han Dynasty. Ends with Jin Dynasty reunification. Source: *Records of the Three Kingdoms* (Sanguozhi) by Chen Shou

3. **Goths invade Roman Empire (251 AD)** — Emperor Decius killed at the Battle of Abritus, first Roman Emperor to die in battle against barbarians. Source: Jordanes, *Getica*

4. **Diocletian becomes Emperor (284 AD)** — Establishes the Tetrarchy (rule by four emperors), restoring stability. Persecutes Christians in the last great imperial persecution. Source: Lactantius, *De Mortibus Persecutorum*

5. **Maya Classic Period begins (~250 AD)** — Major Maya city-states rise in Mesoamerica with advanced writing, astronomy, and architecture. Source: National Geographic; Simon Martin & Nikolai Grube, *Chronicle of the Maya Kings and Queens*
